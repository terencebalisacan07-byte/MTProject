Welcome to Sarrat

The historic heart of Ilocos Norte — known for its rich culture, peaceful community, and timeless traditions.

History of Sarrat

Sarrat is one of the oldest towns in Ilocos Norte. It was founded in the 16th century during the Spanish period. The town is known for its beautiful old church, the Santa Monica Church, which is one of the biggest in the province. Sarrat is also the birthplace of former President Ferdinand E. Marcos. Through the years, the town has kept its rich culture, traditions, and strong community spirit.

Products of Sarrat

The hardworking people of Sarrat produce goods that showcase Ilocano creativity and pride. Some of the most popular local products are:

  • binakol –Binakol is a handwoven cloth made in Sarrat, Ilocos Norte. It has beautiful patterns that look like moving waves or circles. The weavers use wooden looms and cotton threads to make it. Binakol is used for clothes, bags, and home decorations. It shows the skill and creativity of the people of Sarrat.
  • tupig –Tupig is a famous native delicacy from Sarrat, Ilocos Norte. It’s made of glutinous rice, coconut, and brown sugar, wrapped in banana leaves and grilled over charcoal. Tupig from Sarrat is known for its sweet taste and smoky aroma, making it a favorite treat during festivals and special occasions.

Festival of Sarrat

The most awaited celebration in Sarrat is the Sta. Monica Fiesta, held every May in honor of the town’s patron saint, St. Monica. It features colorful parades, street dancing, cultural shows, and food fairs.

The festival unites the community and showcases the faith, hospitality, and joyful spirit of the Sarrateños.
